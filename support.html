❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to the most common citizen questions about NagrikConnect.

Submitting a complaint is simple and takes less than 2 minutes:

  1. Click Submit Complaint from any page
  2. Fill in Step 1: Enter a title, select the category (e.g., Sanitation, Noise), describe the issue, and set the priority
  3. In Step 2: Upload photos as evidence and enter the exact location
  4. Review your complaint in Step 3 and click Submit
  5. You will receive a unique Tracking ID via SMS and on screen

You can also use Voice Input to speak your complaint instead of typing it.

When you submit a complaint, NagrikConnect automatically generates a unique Tracking ID in the format NC-YYYY-XXX (e.g., NC-2024-001). This ID is sent to your registered mobile number via SMS.

To track your complaint:

  1. Go to Track Complaint
  2. Enter your Tracking ID in the search box
  3. Click Track Now to see the full resolution timeline

You do not need to be logged in to track a complaint — just your Tracking ID is sufficient.

Resolution time depends on the priority and category of the complaint:

  • 🔴 Critical (e.g., sewage overflow, water main break): 24–48 hours
  • 🟠 High (e.g., pothole causing accidents, power outage): 3–5 days
  • 🟡 Medium (e.g., broken streetlight, noise): 5–7 days
  • 🟢 Low (e.g., park bench damage): 7–14 days

If a complaint is not resolved within its SLA, it is automatically escalated to a higher authority. The average resolution time across all categories is under 24 hours for acknowledged complaints.

Yes. In Step 2 of the complaint submission form, you will find a checkbox labelled "Submit Anonymously". When selected, your personal details (name, phone number, email) will not be shared with any government authority or officer.

However, we recommend providing contact information so that you can receive status updates and be notified when your complaint is resolved. Your information is always stored securely and is never shared publicly.

NagrikConnect has an automatic Smart Escalation System that monitors all complaints:

  1. Day 1: Complaint submitted and assigned to local ward office
  2. SLA Deadline: If not resolved by deadline, automatic escalation to Municipal Commissioner
  3. +3 Days: Further escalation to District Collector if still unresolved
  4. +7 Days: Escalation to State-level Urban Development Ministry

You will receive an SMS notification at each escalation stage. All escalations are logged publicly for accountability.

The Voice Input feature is specifically designed for citizens who cannot read or write easily. Here's how it works:

  1. On the complaint submission page, select your preferred language from the dropdown
  2. Click the 🎤 Voice Input button next to the description field
  3. A microphone will activate — speak clearly about the issue in your language
  4. The system transcribes your speech to text automatically
  5. Review the transcribed text and make corrections if needed
  6. Submit the form as normal

Voice input works on any modern smartphone — no app download required. It works entirely in the browser.
